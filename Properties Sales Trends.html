Identifying trends and extracting insights from Washington DC Properties sales
    GitHub

Jun 2019 ~ BSc Course "Project Big Data"

Length:   1mo (at 1.0 FTE)

Programming language:   Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, datetime, RE, SciPy, statsmodels)

Data:  150 000 properties in Washington DC, each with 48 attributes, ranging from the area of the property to its heating system, from the sale price to the zip code

Problem description:
Exploring and manipulating big data with the help of Python to extract correlations, trends, and insights about properties sales

Approach & Results:
The data were stored and analyzed with the help of Pandas' dataframe. Prior analysis, the dataframe was cleaned by erasing the columns that appear twice and the ones that have the same entries for all the rows in the data. Next, the outliers were eliminated based on inconsistencies with the data found online (e.g. The largest sale on Google was $63 M, but in the data was $147 M. Thus, the entry was regarded as a mistake and erased). Matplotlib was then used to plot the evolution of the average selling price over time, pointing out that the real estate market increased consistently in the last 30 years.

Moreover, it was found that:
   - the factors that have the highest correlation with the selling price are the gross area of the house (0.34), its condition (0.32), sale date (0.30), grade (0.28), and style (0.23)
   - there is no significant difference in the selling price between the houses in excellent condition and the ones in good condition


   - the selling prices are higher in June compared to other months


   - the 2-story properties are sold the most by far among all types of house
