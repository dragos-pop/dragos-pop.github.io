Research of DataDetective's segmentation algorithm

Jan 2020 ~ Internship at Sentient Information Systems

Length:   2mo (at 1.0 FTE) + 4mo (at 0.5 FTE)

Programming languages:
   - Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, pyodbc, JSON, ctypes, SciPy, datetime, Math, statistics, random)
   - SQL

Data:
   - ACSI campsites dataset that contains various details about camping places, such as region, facilities, clicks, and number of reservations
   - Similarity matrix of the segmentation generated by the DataDetective's segmentation algorithm

Approach:
The first part of the research involved seeking a measure of the similarity matrix that would provide an insight into its relationship with the generated clusters. Then, the possibility of adjusting the similarity matrix was inspected by drastically increasing the number of connections for each record and assigning weights corresponding to a logistic function to the newly added connections.

Results:
   - From the first part, it was found that the more uniform the overlap score distribution, the better the picture
  - Following the second part of the research, it was shown that adding more connections and implementing a weighting scheme that follows a logistic distribution is at least as good as the original version of the algorithm 83% of the times and better 41% of the times
