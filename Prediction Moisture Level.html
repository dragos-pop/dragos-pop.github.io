Predicting the fertilizer's moisture at a crucial point in the process of ICL

Jan 2020 ~ Internship at Sentient Information Systems

Length:   2mo (at 0.5 FTE)

Programming languages:
   - Python (Pandas, datetime, Matplotlib, SciPy, scikit-learn, Keras)
   - SQL

Data:  Multiple time series, each storing an attribute (moisture, temperature, amperage) of the fertilizer batch or the machines at different points in the plant's process

Problem description:
Based on the circa 200 parameters logged on a minute basis, predict the moisture level of the fertilizer at a crucial point in ICL's factory process

Approach & Results:
An Artificial Neural Network was trained with the available data that was previously organized considering the time between the steps in the process (i.e. if the fertilizer batch is processed for 5 minutes in between measuring point A and B, then the measurements of A at time X and measurements of B at X+5 were taken together as they belong to the same batch of fertilizer). Then, the hyperparameters were tuned using Grid Search. Accordingly, the model scored an RMSE of 0.37 on the test data, which was 10.8% better than score obtained by a competitor company (0.41) to whom ACL assigned the same task.
